History of Abu Bakar As-Siddiq

  • Abu Bakar As-Siddiq was born in 573 AD in Mecca, Saudi Arabia. His real name was Abdullah ibn Uthman.
  • He was a close friend and companion of the Prophet Muhammad. They knew each other since their youth.
  • Abu Bakar was the first adult male to accept Islam and was always supportive of Prophet Muhammad.
  • He was known for his wisdom and was often consulted by the Prophet Muhammad on important matters.
  • During the migration to Medina (Hijrah), Abu Bakar accompanied the Prophet Muhammad and faced many hardships.
  • After the death of Prophet Muhammad in 632 AD, Abu Bakar was elected as the first Caliph of the Islamic community.
  • His caliphate lasted for about two years from 632 AD to 634 AD. During his rule, he consolidated the Muslim community and expanded the Islamic state.
  • He was known for his justice and piety. He set a high standard for future caliphs with his simple and righteous life.
  • Abu Bakar passed away in 634 AD and was buried next to Prophet Muhammad in Medina.

    • History of Mahallah Siddiq

    • This Mahallah was established in 1997.
    • With an approximate area of 6.5 acres, this Mahallah has 7 residential blocks that can cater up to 940 residents and 1 central facilities block.
    • This Mahallah was named to commemorate Sayyidina Abu Bakr as-Siddiq RA, his towering personality and his brilliant deeds to Islam and the Muslim Ummah.
    • This Mahallah, like other 16 Mahallat in IIUM Gombak Campus, is under the joint care of Residential and Services Department and Student Affairs and Development Division.
